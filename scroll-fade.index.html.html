CHAPTER 01

The Beginning

Meet Jane. She's 24 years old, fresh out of college with a degree in marketing. She loves hiking, cooking with friends, and practicing yoga every Sunday morning.

Jane is happy. She has real relationships. She sleeps 8 hours a night. She feels alive.

8h sleep/night 3 gym sessions/week Weekly brunches with friends

Then, one day, she posts a photo from her hike. It goes viral. 10,000 likes. Her DMs explode with brand deals.

"Maybe I could make this work," she thinks.

CHAPTER 02

The Rise

Jane quits her job. She's going to be a full-time influencer.

At first, it's exciting. Brands send her free products. She attends exclusive events. Her follower count climbs daily. The money starts flowing in.

50K followers $3,000/sponsored post 5-8 posts per day

But something changes. She starts checking her phone every 5 minutes. She films everything. Her friends notice she's always "working."

"I'll just check my notifications real quick," becomes her most common phrase.

She doesn't notice she's stopped calling her mom. She doesn't notice she's eating every meal while staring at analytics. She doesn't notice she's becoming someone else.

CHAPTER 03

The Price of Fame

Jane now has 500K followers. She's making $15,000 a month. She's "successful."

But she can't remember the last time she saw her friends. She hasn't been to the gym in months. She sleeps 4 hours a night because she's optimizing post timing for engagement in different time zones.

500K followers 4h sleep/night 12h screen time/day

Dark circles under her eyes. Weight gain from stress eating. Constant anxiety about the algorithm.

You Can Break Free

The people who built these systems are now fighting against them. Aza Raskin, who invented infinite scroll, calls it his biggest regret. Tristan Harris, former Google design ethicist, dedicates his life to exposing these tactics.

You don't have to be the next Jane. Here's how to start:

Immediate Actions You Can Take Today

  • Turn off ALL non-essential notifications right now
  • Remove social media apps from your phone home screen
  • Enable Screen Time limits (2 hours max for social media)
  • Switch your phone to grayscale mode to reduce dopamine triggers
  • Delete at least one social media app for 7 days (just try it)
  • Set "Do Not Disturb" as your default mode
  • Unfollow accounts that make you feel inadequate
  • Schedule "phone-free" time blocks daily
